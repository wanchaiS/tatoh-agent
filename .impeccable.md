## Design Context

### Users
Two distinct audiences sharing one product:

**Guests** — on phone or tablet, relaxed, exploring the resort. They interact through the chat interface. They're on vacation: sensory-aware, easily turned off by anything corporate or clunky.

**Staff** — front desk, concierge, operations. Desktop, during shifts. Need density and reliability.

### Brand Personality
**Quietly luxurious.** Cooper should feel like the resort itself: refined without announcing itself, precise without being cold.

Three-word anchor: **composed · precise · warm**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark. Deep warm charcoal surfaces, cream text, warm gold accent.
- **Palette (OKLCH)**:
  - Background: `oklch(0.18 0.01 60)` — deep warm charcoal
  - Text: `oklch(0.92 0.015 80)` — cream white
  - Accent: `oklch(0.75 0.12 75)` — warm gold
  - Border: `oklch(0.28 0.01 60)` — subtle charcoal lift
  - Muted: `oklch(0.55 0.01 70)` — mid-gray warm
- **Previous palette is REPLACED** — do not build on the warm cream / eucalyptus green system
- **Reference**: Linear/Notion density + luxury hospitality warmth. NOT generic SaaS dashboards.
- **Anti-reference**: The current cream-background look. Anything that reads as enterprise property management software.

### Mascot
The AI is represented by a **2D pixel art cat** on the main chat page (`/`).
- Idle state: subtle breathing or blinking animation
- Talking state: animated (mouth moving, tail flicking, ears perking) while AI response is streaming
- Pixel art style: ~32×32 or 48×48 grid, warm gold/cream palette matching the brand
- Positioned near the chat input or as a central character in the empty-state view
- The mascot gives the chat page personality and fills the currently empty feel

### Design Principles
1. **Premium through restraint.** Dark surfaces + warm gold. No glows, no gradients, no neon.
2. **The mascot is the personality.** Let the pixel cat carry the warmth; the UI around it stays clean.
3. **Density for staff, breathing room for guests.** Knowledge management views tight; chat interface open.
4. **Typography carries the brand signal.** Choose a font that feels editorial and considered, not a developer default (no Inter, no Geist, no Syne).
5. **Trustworthy first.** Every layout decision should make the product feel solid and reliable, not playful or casual.
